# Summary Report: FCAJ Community Day

### Event Information:
* **Event Name:** FCAJ Community Day

---

### Event Objectives:
* **Networking & Inspiration:** Provide an open space for attendees to connect, inspire each other, and actively build professional networks and relationships.

* **Empowering Young Talents:** Help students and junior engineers understand the shifting IT job market in the AI era, encouraging them to focus on building "real products" rather than simple demos.

### Key Highlights:

#### 1. Shift in the IT Job Market
* As AI dramatically reduces software development costs, the demand for custom software creation will spike exponentially.
* This trend creates new job roles, such as platform engineering and specialized debugging of AI-generated code.

#### 2. AI Contextual Challenges & Randomness
* **Context is Everything:** LLMs often produce generic or irrelevant output when users dump requests without proper localized context.
* **Inference Randomness:** Even with temperature set to 0, LLMs hosted on cloud systems can return varying outputs due to inference optimization layers implemented by cloud providers.

#### 3. Startup Credit Rating Challenges
* Assessing creditworthiness for startups is challenging as they lack traditional collaterals or three-year financial statements.

#### 4. Solutions Introduced
* **Amazon CloudFront Flat-rate Pricing:** A pricing structure helping businesses avoid unpredictable billing spikes when using Content Delivery Networks (CDN).
* **Multi-Agent Systems:** Deploying multiple specialized AI agents playing specific roles (e.g., financial analyst, market researcher) to solve complex business flows.
* **Core Technologies:** Amazon Bedrock Agent Core, Amazon Q (for internal agents), Amazon S3, Terraform, and AWS CloudFront.

#### 5. Live Demos & Case Studies
* **Amazon Q Revenue Analysis:** Demo of Amazon Q automatically parsing Excel financial sheets and generating a clean, interactive dashboard.
* **UTM Morpho Application:** Demo of an AI tool converting wireframe drawings into clean HTML/CSS code, allowing live edits via a drag-and-drop UI.
* **Comparison Case Study:** Analyzing the differences in text outputs of LLMs hosted on Amazon Bedrock versus locally under temperature=0.
* **Enterprise Security Standards:** Deploying AI in enterprises requires strict guardrails, anti-prompt injection, auditing, and high-standard security compliance.

---

### Key Takeaways:

#### 1. Design Mindset & Methodologies
* Always start with business questions first: *"Who is using this, what are they using it for, why must they use it, and when is the right time?"*
* **Hackathon Strategy:** Focus on perfecting one core feature rather than overloading the project with too many incomplete features (avoid overthinking).

#### 2. Technical Learnings
* Deep dive into CloudFront architecture (VPC Origin, mTLS).
* Gained insights into LLM next-token selection principles (logits and probabilities).
* **Testing:** Emphasized the importance of rigorous testing with edge cases to control LLM outputs and avoid over-generation.

---

### Applying to Work:
* **Contextual Prompting:** Implement strict role configurations and contextual boundaries for development assistants to yield better code output.
* **Tooling Experiments:** Pilot Amazon Bedrock Agent Core for multi-agent building, and utilize Terraform as Infrastructure as Code (IaC) for environment automation.
* **Security & Audits:** Configure Guardrails and Audit Trails to evaluate and track AI decisions before pushing them to Production.

---

### Event Experience:
* **Career Insights:** Gained practical wisdom from speakers discussing non-traditional career paths (working without a university degree) and dealing with bank compliance.
* **Hands-on Demos:** Saw real-time UI streaming and automated dashboards generated by Amazon Q.
* **Networking:** Exchanged valuable ideas, project architectures, and contact info with other developers.
* **Memorable Case Study:** Learnt about an incident where a developer copied buggy ChatGPT code straight to a bank's Production system, triggering a full-scale code audit.

---

### Key Lessons & Next Steps:
* **Enterprise Standards:** Enterprise systems are completely different from PoCs or school projects; they demand high security, strict compliance, and solid software engineering.
* **Competitive Edge:** Soft skills, English proficiency, practical domain knowledge, and a university degree remain vital differentiators in the job market.
* **Future Focus:** Shift learning focus from shallow AI wrappers to core Software Engineering, Backend principles, and AI adoption strategies.
