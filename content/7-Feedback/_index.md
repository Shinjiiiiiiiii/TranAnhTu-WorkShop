Here are my personal reflections and constructive feedback after completing my journey with the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program:

### 1. Learning & Working Environment
The program provides an incredibly dynamic and collaborative ecosystem for students nationwide who are passionate about cloud technology. While the learning curve was steep and the pace was fast initially, the welcoming community made it easy to adapt. Although mostly virtual, discussions on Slack and Discord were highly active, and peers and coordinators were always ready to assist with technical blockers.

### 2. Guidance from Mentors & Administrative Support
I highly appreciate the mentoring style in this program. Instead of simply providing the solutions to configuration bugs, my mentor guided me through reading error logs, searching official AWS documentation, and troubleshooting independently. This approach has greatly improved my analytical thinking. The administrative team was also very supportive, ensuring smooth communications and quick handling of programmatic requirements.

### 3. Relevance to My Major (Machine Learning & Applications)
As a student majoring in Machine Learning and Applications at **Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HUTECH)**, this training was the perfect bridge. I used to write machine learning models locally; now, through FCAJ, I understand how to deploy and scale them on AWS using Serverless microservices, Docker packaging, and container orchestration on Amazon ECS/Fargate.

### 4. What I Found Most Satisfying
* Direct access to real AWS Cloud resources to practice labs and build actual infrastructure projects.
* Huge growth in self-learning habits and structured technical writing skills.
* Building a network of talented peers who share similar career goals in Cloud and DevOps.

### 5. Suggestions for Future Cohorts
* **More Live Troubleshooting Labs**: It would be highly beneficial to introduce a few live lab sessions with mentors focused on fixing common configuration mistakes (e.g., IAM permission errors or VPC subnet routing blocks).
* **Social Sessions**: Organizing brief virtual meetups or informal coffee chats would help participants connect more closely beyond purely technical tasks.

### 6. Conclusion
If a fellow student asked me about joining the First Cloud AI Journey, I would recommend it without hesitation. It is a fantastic bridge to cover the gap between academic theory and real-world engineering demands.
